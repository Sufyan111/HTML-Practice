Values of Industries to a Country

The industrial progress of a country is one way to assess its development. A country, that is not industrially advanced,cannot be considered strong. Although agriculture provides the basic necessities of life, the maximising of agricultural produce is possible only through sustained growth.


The modern equipments required for mass production in agriculture are to be manufactured in modernised industries. In India, the industrial sector contributes to more than half of our national income. The consumer items used in daily life such as soaps, toothpaste, medicines etc., are produced in large quantities, cater to a very large population and are manufactured in industries. this mass production is required to meet the demand and maintain prices with respect to the purchasing power of the people.


India is a large exporter of various items such as textiles, food grains, medicines, etc., Therefore, our products must conform to international standards. Only superior quality and competitive prices can find a place in the international market.


Industrialization alone can contribute to national security. There are no two opinions about this. Several ordinance factories are established to provide the security personnel with weapons. Regular updation of these industries is necessary to meet the growing need to improve the war equipment. or else it would lead to stagnation of technology. Today, Insia is responsible nuclear power. This was possible because of the constant industrial development since the time of Pandit Nehru.


There is also a harmful side of Industrialization. These are related to pollution. Pollution of ground water, rivers, lakes, atmosphere and the sea. Add to it the growing population around the industrial belt. These problems must be addressed very urgently. Or else the progress achieved would be offset by national ill-health!
